The Mets played the Atlanta Braves on Sept 21, 2001
It was the first baseball game to be played after the incident of 9/11.

THE HEALING HOME RUN

The country was traumatized but still strong.
Firefighters, NYPD, and supporters all came together in unity.

The Mets were trailing by 2-1 and in the eighth inning, IT HAPPENED
Mike Piazza scored a 2-run homer taking the Mets to a score of 3-2.

This game brought the whole stadium and the whole of NY together. It is said that it even started the healing process for NY, a moment lived only a few times. Emotions ran high, and people hugged each other and said, "Everything will be all right".
